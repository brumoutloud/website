Frequently Asked Questions

Where is Birmingham's Gay Village located?

Right in the city centre, bab! It's centred around Hurst Street, just a hop, skip, and a jump away from New Street Station.

What are the best gay bars and clubs in Birmingham?

Where do we start? The Village Inn, The Fox, The Nightingale Club, Sidewalk, Missing Bar, The Loft... the list goes on! Check out our venues page for all the deets.

Is Birmingham safe for LGBTQ+ visitors?

Generally, yes. Like any city, it's always good to be aware of your surroundings, but Birmingham is a welcoming place for LGBTQ+ folks.

Is there a gay sauna in Birmingham?

None in the city centre but there are a few options if you don't mind a short journey. The Greenhouse in Darlaston is a popular choice, boasting a swimming pool alongside its other facilities, perfect for a refreshing dip after a steam. For those who prefer to stay closer to Birmingham, Spartan in Erdington offers another welcoming space to unwind and relax. Heroes in Stourbridge is also worth checking out if you fancy a bit of a road trip.

How does Birmingham's gay village compare to Soho, Manchester or Brighton?

Let's set the record straight: Birmingham's queer scene is a force to be reckoned with! Our Gay Village boasts a vibrant array of bars and clubs, rivaling the likes of Manchester's Canal Street, Brighton's Kemptown, and even London's Soho. And that's just the tip of the iceberg! Across the West Midlands, you'll find a whole host of LGBTQ+ venues and events, from cosy pubs to legendary clubs, drag shows to queer film festivals. We might not always get the spotlight, but we're proud to be a hidden gem of LGBTQ+ life in the UK.
